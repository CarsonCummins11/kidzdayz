Arcos de la Frontera

Saturday, July 8, 2017

Arcos de la Frontera is one of Andalucia's most dramatically positioned white villages. Arcos balances atop a rocky limestone ridge, its whitewashed houses and stone castle walls stopping abruptly as a sheer cliff face plunges down to the fertile valley of the river Guadalete below.  We stayed in a hotel that was built as a private home in 1729.  We were alone on the basement level with the two rooms with amazing balconies.  We could actually watch the birds flying below us from our balconies!  This was a very special place - again a serious contender for trip favorite!
